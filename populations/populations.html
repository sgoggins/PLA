Background

The cross-section of students taking a course and their performance is a basic question.

Analysis

Treemaps provide a compact way to visually display rich, hierarchical information. For several terms of students taking Physics I, we ask "what were their eventual majors and mean grades?" Cell size in the treeemap indicates the total proportion by major, and the mean grade is contained in color coding. We also label majors by division (engineering, humanities, professional, social science, science)

Our code reduces the student course and student record tables to frequncy tables formatted for the R treemap package to produce plots like you see here, and additional treemaps that show the typical courses taken previously, concurrently, and after a given course.
