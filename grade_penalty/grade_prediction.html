Background

Using grades as measures of performance, we ask the question "which students do not perform as expected?" We can reshape and address this question in various forms. As a first pass, grade point average in other classes (GPAO) forms an expectation for how a student should perform, and the difference between the actual grade and GPA in other classes measures a "grade anomaly" or "grade penalty".

Results

We can compare these between groups, for instance,males and females in a course:

In Physics 135, we see that the simple grade penalty (grade-GPAO) is larger for females than males (-0.32 vs. -0.13), and that this difference is not covered by the standard error. The difference is seen for students at most GPAOs, and regression models that attempt to control for confounding variables do not elimnate this gender-associated gap. Optimal matching on HSGPA, and entrance exam scores reduces, but does not eliminate this gap either.

Finally, the analysis code the produces these results can be found here, where details on regression, matching, and estimation of standard errors are contained in the code. The associated wiki gives details on how to run the code.
